Biodiversity of Animals

1. Definition of Biodiversity

Biodiversity refers to the variety of living organisms in an ecosystem.

2. Classification of Animals

Animals are classified into two main groups:

  • Invertebrates (no backbone)
  • Vertebrates (have backbone)

3. Invertebrates

3.1 Porifera (Sponges)

  • Asymmetrical body
  • No true tissues
  • Aquatic

3.2 Cnidaria (e.g. jellyfish)

  • Radial symmetry
  • Have tentacles with stinging cells
  • Aquatic

3.3 Platyhelminthes (Flatworms)

  • Flat body
  • Bilateral symmetry
  • No body cavity

3.4 Nematoda (Roundworms)

  • Cylindrical body
  • Unsegmented

3.5 Annelida (Segmented worms)

  • Segmented body
  • Closed circulatory system

3.6 Arthropoda

  • Jointed legs
  • Exoskeleton
  • Segmented body

Examples:

  • Insects
  • Arachnids
  • Crustaceans

3.7 Mollusca

  • Soft body
  • Often have a shell

3.8 Echinodermata

  • Radial symmetry (adults)
  • Spiny skin
  • Marine animals

4. Vertebrates

4.1 Fish

  • Gills for respiration
  • Fins for movement
  • Cold-blooded (ectothermic)

4.2 Amphibians

  • Live in water and land
  • Moist skin
  • External fertilisation

4.3 Reptiles

  • Dry, scaly skin
  • Internal fertilisation
  • Lay eggs

4.4 Birds

  • Feathers
  • Wings
  • Lay eggs

4.5 Mammals

  • Hair or fur
  • Mammary glands
  • Give birth to live young

5. Key Classification Features

  • Body symmetry
  • Presence of a backbone
  • Type of body covering
  • Method of reproduction
  • Habitat

6. Differences Between Vertebrates and Invertebrates

Vertebrates Invertebrates
Have backbone No backbone
More complex Simpler

7. Importance of Animal Biodiversity

  • Maintains ecological balance
  • Supports food chains
  • Provides resources for humans

8. Threats to Animal Biodiversity

  • Habitat destruction
  • Pollution
  • Overhunting
  • Climate change

9. Conservation of Animals

  • Protected areas
  • Breeding programmes
  • Laws to protect species

10. Quick Summary

  • Animals are classified into vertebrates and invertebrates
  • Each group has unique characteristics
  • Biodiversity is important for ecosystem stability
