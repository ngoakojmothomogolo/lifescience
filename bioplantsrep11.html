Biodiversity of Plants and Reproduction

1. Biodiversity of Plants

Plant biodiversity refers to the variety of plant species in an ecosystem.

2. Classification of Plants

2.1 Bryophytes (Mosses)

  • Non-vascular plants
  • No true roots, stems or leaves
  • Reproduce by spores
  • Require water for reproduction

2.2 Pteridophytes (Ferns)

  • Vascular plants
  • Have roots, stems and leaves
  • Reproduce by spores
  • No seeds

2.3 Gymnosperms

  • Have seeds
  • No flowers
  • Seeds are exposed (cones)

2.4 Angiosperms

  • Flowering plants
  • Seeds enclosed in fruit

Types:

  • Monocotyledons
  • Dicotyledons

3. Differences Between Monocots and Dicots

Monocots Dicots
One cotyledon Two cotyledons
Parallel veins Network veins
Fibrous roots Tap root system

4. Asexual Reproduction in Plants

Asexual reproduction involves one parent and produces genetically identical offspring.

4.1 Methods:

  • Runners (stolons)
  • Bulbs
  • Tubers
  • Rhizomes

Advantages:

  • Fast reproduction
  • No need for pollination

Disadvantages:

  • No genetic variation

5. Sexual Reproduction in Plants

Sexual reproduction involves fusion of male and female gametes.

6. Structure of a Flower

  • Sepals – protect the bud
  • Petals – attract pollinators
  • Stamen (male):
    • Anther – produces pollen
    • Filament – supports anther
  • Pistil/Carpel (female):
    • Stigma – receives pollen
    • Style – connects stigma to ovary
    • Ovary – contains ovules

7. Pollination

Pollination is the transfer of pollen from the anther to the stigma.

7.1 Types:

  • Self-pollination
  • Cross-pollination

8. Fertilisation

  1. Pollen lands on stigma
  2. Pollen tube grows down the style
  3. Male gamete travels down pollen tube
  4. Fuses with female gamete in ovule
  5. Zygote is formed

9. Seed and Fruit Formation

  • Ovule develops into seed
  • Ovary develops into fruit

10. Seed Structure

  • Seed coat
  • Embryo
  • Food store (cotyledons)

11. Germination

  1. Seed absorbs water
  2. Enzymes are activated
  3. Radicle emerges (root)
  4. Plumule develops (shoot)

Conditions for germination:

  • Water
  • Oxygen
  • Suitable temperature

12. Importance of Plant Reproduction

  • Ensures survival of species
  • Maintains biodiversity

13. Quick Summary

  • Plants are classified into bryophytes, pteridophytes, gymnosperms and angiosperms
  • Reproduction can be sexual or asexual
  • Pollination leads to fertilisation
  • Seeds develop into new plants
