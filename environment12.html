Human Impact on the Environment

1. Introduction

Human activities have a significant impact on the environment.

These impacts can lead to environmental degradation and loss of biodiversity.

2. Loss of Biodiversity

Biodiversity refers to the variety of living organisms in an ecosystem.

Causes:

  • Habitat destruction
  • Pollution
  • Climate change
  • Overexploitation

Effects:

  • Extinction of species
  • Disruption of ecosystems

3. Habitat Destruction

Natural habitats are destroyed due to human activities.

Examples:

  • Deforestation
  • Urbanisation
  • Mining

Effects:

  • Loss of species
  • Reduced biodiversity

4. Pollution

4.1 Air Pollution

Release of harmful gases into the atmosphere.

Causes:

  • Burning fossil fuels
  • Industrial emissions

Effects:

  • Respiratory diseases
  • Global warming

4.2 Water Pollution

Contamination of water bodies.

Causes:

  • Sewage disposal
  • Chemical waste

Effects:

  • Death of aquatic organisms
  • Eutrophication

4.3 Land Pollution

  • Improper waste disposal
  • Use of pesticides

5. Eutrophication (VERY IMPORTANT)

Process (CAPS style):

  1. Fertilisers enter water bodies
  2. Nutrient levels increase
  3. Algae grow rapidly (algal bloom)
  4. Light is blocked from reaching aquatic plants
  5. Plants die
  6. Decomposers break down dead plants
  7. Oxygen is used up
  8. Aquatic organisms die due to lack of oxygen

6. Global Warming

Global warming is the increase in Earth's average temperature.

Cause: Increase in greenhouse gases (CO₂, methane)

Effects:

  • Climate change
  • Melting ice caps
  • Rising sea levels

7. Greenhouse Effect

Process:

  1. Sunlight enters the atmosphere
  2. Earth absorbs heat
  3. Heat is radiated back
  4. Greenhouse gases trap heat
  5. Temperature increases

8. Ozone Layer Depletion

The ozone layer protects Earth from harmful UV radiation.

Cause: CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons)

Effects:

  • Skin cancer
  • Eye damage
  • Damage to plants

9. Waste Management

Types of waste:

  • Biodegradable
  • Non-biodegradable

Methods:

  • Recycling
  • Reusing
  • Reducing waste

10. Conservation

Conservation is the protection and sustainable use of natural resources.

Methods:

  • Protected areas (national parks)
  • Breeding programmes
  • Laws and regulations

11. Summary (Exam Ready)

  • Human activities affect the environment negatively
  • Pollution leads to ecosystem damage
  • Eutrophication reduces oxygen in water
  • Greenhouse gases cause global warming
  • Conservation helps protect biodiversity
