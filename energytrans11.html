Energy Transformations in Living Organisms

1. Energy and Metabolism

Energy: The ability to do work.

Metabolism: All chemical reactions that occur in a cell.

  • Anabolism: Building up reactions (require energy)
  • Catabolism: Breaking down reactions (release energy)

2. ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)

ATP is the energy carrier in cells.

  • Stores energy in phosphate bonds
  • Energy is released when ATP → ADP + P

Function of ATP:

  • Provides energy for metabolic processes

3. Enzymes

Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions.

Characteristics:

  • Specific to substrate
  • Not used up in reactions

Factors affecting enzyme activity:

  • Temperature
  • pH
  • Substrate concentration
  • Enzyme concentration

Lock and Key Model:

  • Substrate fits into enzyme's active site
  • Enzyme-substrate complex forms

4. Photosynthesis

Definition: Process by which green plants use light energy to convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen.

Balanced Equation:

6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ (in the presence of light and chlorophyll)

Requirements:

  • Light
  • Chlorophyll
  • Carbon dioxide
  • Water

Process

  1. Light energy is absorbed by chlorophyll
  2. Light energy is converted to chemical energy
  3. Water is split into hydrogen and oxygen
  4. Hydrogen combines with carbon dioxide to form glucose
  5. Oxygen is released as a by-product

Importance:

  • Produces food (glucose)
  • Releases oxygen
  • Stores energy

5. Cellular Respiration

Definition: Process by which glucose is broken down to release energy.

Balanced Equation:

C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ENERGY (ATP)

Types of Respiration:

Aerobic Respiration

  • Requires oxygen
  • Produces large amount of ATP

Process (CAPS style):

  1. Glucose is broken down
  2. Oxygen is used
  3. Carbon dioxide and water are produced
  4. Energy is released as ATP

Anaerobic Respiration

  • Occurs without oxygen
  • Produces less ATP

In humans:

  • Glucose → Lactic acid + Energy

In yeast:

  • Glucose → Alcohol + Carbon dioxide + Energy

6. Differences Between Photosynthesis and Respiration

Photosynthesis Respiration
Occurs in chloroplast Occurs in mitochondria
Requires light Does not require light
Produces glucose Breaks down glucose
Releases oxygen Uses oxygen

7. Relationship Between Photosynthesis and Respiration

  • Products of photosynthesis are reactants of respiration
  • Products of respiration are reactants of photosynthesis

8. Importance of Energy Transformation

  • Ensures survival of organisms
  • Provides energy for life processes
  • Maintains ecosystems

9. Quick Summary

  • ATP is the energy carrier
  • Photosynthesis stores energy
  • Respiration releases energy
  • Enzymes control metabolic reactions
