Biodiversity and Classification of Micro-organisms

1. Definition of Biodiversity

Biodiversity refers to the variety of living organisms in an ecosystem.

Includes:

  • Genetic diversity
  • Species diversity
  • Ecosystem diversity

2. Importance of Biodiversity

  • Maintains ecosystem stability
  • Provides food, medicine and resources
  • Ensures survival of species

3. Classification of Organisms

Classification is the grouping of organisms based on similarities and differences.

4. Reasons for Classification

  • To organise organisms
  • To identify organisms
  • To show evolutionary relationships

5. Levels of Classification (Taxonomic Hierarchy)

  • Kingdom
  • Phylum
  • Class
  • Order
  • Family
  • Genus
  • Species

6. Binomial Nomenclature

  • Scientific naming system
  • Uses two names: Genus and species
  • Example: Homo sapiens
  • Genus starts with capital letter
  • Species starts with small letter

7. Five Kingdom System

  • Monera
  • Protista
  • Fungi
  • Plantae
  • Animalia

8. Classification of Micro-organisms

Micro-organisms are organisms that are too small to be seen with the naked eye.

8.1 Viruses

  • Non-cellular
  • Contain genetic material (DNA or RNA)
  • Require a host to reproduce
  • Cause diseases

8.2 Bacteria (Kingdom Monera)

  • Unicellular
  • No nucleus (prokaryotic)
  • Cell wall present
  • Reproduce by binary fission

Importance:

  • Useful in decomposition
  • Some cause diseases

8.3 Protists (Kingdom Protista)

  • Mostly unicellular
  • Have a nucleus (eukaryotic)

Examples:

  • Amoeba
  • Paramecium

8.4 Fungi (Kingdom Fungi)

  • Multicellular (except yeast)
  • Cell walls made of chitin
  • Feed by absorption
  • Reproduce by spores

Examples:

  • Mushrooms
  • Moulds
  • Yeast

9. Differences Between Prokaryotic and Eukaryotic Cells

Prokaryotic Eukaryotic
No nucleus Has nucleus
No membrane-bound organelles Has organelles
Small and simple Larger and complex

10. Importance of Micro-organisms

  • Decomposition
  • Food production (e.g. bread, yoghurt)
  • Medicine (antibiotics)

11. Harmful Effects of Micro-organisms

  • Cause diseases
  • Food spoilage

12. Diseases Caused by Micro-organisms

  • Tuberculosis (bacteria)
  • Cholera (bacteria)
  • Malaria (protist)
  • HIV/AIDS (virus)

13. Control of Micro-organisms

  • Hygiene
  • Vaccination
  • Use of antibiotics

14. Quick Summary

  • Biodiversity includes all living organisms
  • Classification groups organisms based on similarities
  • Micro-organisms include viruses, bacteria, protists and fungi
  • Some are useful, others are harmful
