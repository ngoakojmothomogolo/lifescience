Human Impact on the Environment

1. Definition

Human impact on the environment refers to the negative and positive effects that human activities have on the natural environment.

2. Types of Human Impact

  • Pollution
  • Deforestation
  • Climate change
  • Loss of biodiversity

3. Pollution

3.1 Air Pollution

  • Caused by burning fossil fuels
  • Releases gases such as carbon dioxide and sulphur dioxide

Effects:

  • Respiratory diseases
  • Global warming
  • Acid rain

3.2 Water Pollution

  • Caused by sewage, chemicals and oil spills

Effects:

  • Kills aquatic organisms
  • Contaminates drinking water

3.3 Land Pollution

  • Caused by waste dumping and chemicals

Effects:

  • Soil degradation
  • Loss of fertility

4. Deforestation

  • Cutting down of trees

Causes:

  • Agriculture
  • Urbanisation

Effects:

  • Loss of habitats
  • Soil erosion
  • Increase in carbon dioxide

5. Loss of Biodiversity

  • Extinction of species

Causes:

  • Habitat destruction
  • Pollution
  • Overexploitation

6. Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term changes in temperature and weather patterns.

Causes:

  • Greenhouse gases
  • Burning fossil fuels

6.1 Greenhouse Effect

  1. Sun's energy reaches the Earth
  2. Some energy is absorbed
  3. Some is reflected back
  4. Greenhouse gases trap heat
  5. Temperature increases

6.2 Effects of Climate Change

  • Rising temperatures
  • Droughts
  • Flooding
  • Melting ice caps

7. Ozone Layer Depletion

  • Caused by CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons)

Effects:

  • Increased UV radiation
  • Skin cancer

8. Waste Management

  • Reduce
  • Reuse
  • Recycle

9. Conservation of the Environment

  • Protected areas
  • Sustainable use of resources
  • Environmental education

10. Sustainable Development

Sustainable development is the use of resources in a way that meets present needs without harming future generations.

11. Importance of Conservation

  • Protects biodiversity
  • Maintains ecosystems
  • Ensures future resources

12. Quick Summary

  • Human activities affect the environment
  • Pollution and deforestation are major causes
  • Climate change is a global issue
  • Conservation is essential
