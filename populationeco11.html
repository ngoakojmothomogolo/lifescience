Population Ecology

1. Definition of Population

A population is a group of organisms of the same species living in the same area at the same time.

2. Population Size

Population size is the number of individuals in a population.

Determined by:

  • Birth rate (natality)
  • Death rate (mortality)
  • Immigration
  • Emigration

3. Factors Affecting Population Size

3.1 Density-Dependent Factors

  • Competition
  • Predation
  • Disease

3.2 Density-Independent Factors

  • Drought
  • Floods
  • Temperature extremes

4. Population Density

Population density is the number of individuals per unit area.

Formula:

Population density = Number of individuals / Area

5. Methods of Estimating Population Size

5.1 Direct Counting

  • Used for small populations

5.2 Sampling Methods

Quadrat Method (Plants):

  • Random quadrats placed in area
  • Organisms counted in each quadrat

Mark-Recapture Method (Animals):

Formula:

Population size = (Number marked × Total caught second time) / Number recaptured

6. Population Growth

6.1 Exponential Growth (J-shaped curve)

  • Rapid increase in population
  • Occurs when resources are unlimited

6.2 Logistic Growth (S-shaped curve)

  • Growth slows as resources become limited

Carrying Capacity:

  • Maximum population size the environment can support

7. Population Growth Phases

  • Lag phase – slow growth
  • Exponential phase – rapid growth
  • Stationary phase – growth stabilises

8. Age Structure of Population

  • Pre-reproductive
  • Reproductive
  • Post-reproductive

Importance:

  • Predicts future population growth

9. Survival Curves

  • Type I: Low death rate (humans)
  • Type II: Constant death rate
  • Type III: High death rate early in life

10. Human Population Growth

  • Rapid increase due to improved healthcare
  • Better food supply

Problems:

  • Overpopulation
  • Resource depletion
  • Pollution

11. Demographic Transition Model

  • Stage 1: High birth and death rates
  • Stage 2: High birth rate, low death rate
  • Stage 3: Declining birth rate
  • Stage 4: Low birth and death rates

12. Impact of Humans on Environment

  • Deforestation
  • Pollution
  • Loss of biodiversity

13. Importance of Population Ecology

  • Helps manage resources
  • Helps conservation efforts
  • Predicts population changes

14. Quick Summary

  • Population size changes due to births, deaths, immigration and emigration
  • Population growth can be exponential or logistic
  • Carrying capacity limits population size
  • Sampling methods estimate population size
