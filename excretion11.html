Excretion in Humans

1. Definition of Excretion

Excretion is the removal of metabolic waste products from the body.

Examples of metabolic wastes:

  • Carbon dioxide
  • Urea
  • Excess water
  • Salts

2. Human Excretory Organs

  • Kidneys – remove urea, excess water and salts
  • Lungs – remove carbon dioxide and water vapour
  • Skin – removes sweat (water + salts)
  • Liver – converts ammonia to urea

3. Structure of the Urinary System

  • Kidneys
  • Ureters
  • Urinary bladder
  • Urethra

Functions:

  • Kidneys filter blood
  • Ureters carry urine to bladder
  • Bladder stores urine
  • Urethra releases urine

4. Structure of the Kidney

  • Cortex – outer region
  • Medulla – inner region
  • Pelvis – collects urine

5. Nephron (Functional Unit of Kidney)

  • Bowman's capsule
  • Glomerulus
  • Proximal convoluted tubule
  • Loop of Henle
  • Distal convoluted tubule
  • Collecting duct

6. Formation of Urine

6.1 Glomerular Filtration (Ultrafiltration)

  1. Blood enters glomerulus under high pressure
  2. Small molecules are forced out of blood
  3. These enter Bowman's capsule
  4. Large molecules (proteins, blood cells) remain in blood

Filtrate contains:

  • Water
  • Glucose
  • Salts
  • Urea

6.2 Selective Reabsorption

  1. Useful substances are reabsorbed from filtrate
  2. Glucose is completely reabsorbed
  3. Some salts are reabsorbed
  4. Water is reabsorbed
  5. Substances move back into blood capillaries

6.3 Tubular Secretion

  1. Additional wastes are actively transported into tubule
  2. Includes excess salts and toxins

6.4 Final Urine Contains:

  • Urea
  • Excess water
  • Salts

7. Osmoregulation

Osmoregulation is the control of water and salt balance in the body.

7.1 Role of ADH (Antidiuretic Hormone)

When body is dehydrated:

  1. ADH is released
  2. Kidneys reabsorb more water
  3. Less urine is produced
  4. Urine is concentrated

When body has excess water:

  1. Less ADH is released
  2. Less water is reabsorbed
  3. More urine is produced
  4. Urine is dilute

8. Homeostasis

Homeostasis is the maintenance of a constant internal environment.

  • Kidneys help maintain water balance
  • Regulates salt concentration

9. Kidney Diseases

  • Kidney failure
  • Kidney stones

Treatment:

  • Dialysis
  • Kidney transplant

10. Dialysis

  1. Blood is removed from the body
  2. Passed through dialysis machine
  3. Waste products diffuse into dialysis fluid
  4. Clean blood is returned to the body

11. Differences Between Excretion and Egestion

Excretion Egestion
Removal of metabolic waste Removal of undigested food
Occurs in kidneys, lungs, skin Occurs in digestive system

12. Importance of Excretion

  • Removes toxic substances
  • Maintains internal balance
  • Prevents poisoning of cells

13. Quick Summary

  • Nephron is the functional unit of the kidney
  • Urine formation involves filtration, reabsorption and secretion
  • ADH controls water balance
  • Excretion maintains homeostasis
