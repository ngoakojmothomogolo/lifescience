Gaseous Exchange

1. Definition of Gaseous Exchange

Gaseous exchange is the process by which oxygen diffuses into the body and carbon dioxide diffuses out of the body.

2. Requirements for Efficient Gaseous Exchange Surface

  • Large surface area
  • Thin surface for rapid diffusion
  • Moist surface
  • Good blood supply
  • Steep concentration gradient

3. Gaseous Exchange in Humans

3.1 Structure of the Respiratory System

  • Nasal cavity
  • Pharynx
  • Larynx
  • Trachea
  • Bronchi
  • Bronchioles
  • Alveoli
  • Lungs

Functions:

  • Nasal cavity filters, warms and moistens air
  • Trachea supported by cartilage rings
  • Bronchi branch into bronchioles
  • Alveoli are the site of gaseous exchange

3.2 Structure of the Alveolus

  • Large surface area
  • Thin walls (one cell thick)
  • Moist surface
  • Surrounded by capillaries

3.3 Gaseous Exchange at the Alveoli

  1. Oxygen concentration is higher in alveoli than in blood
  2. Oxygen diffuses from alveoli into blood capillaries
  3. Carbon dioxide concentration is higher in blood than in alveoli
  4. Carbon dioxide diffuses from blood into alveoli
  5. Oxygen binds with haemoglobin to form oxyhaemoglobin

4. Transport of Gases in the Blood

4.1 Transport of Oxygen

  • Oxygen is transported by haemoglobin in red blood cells
  • Forms oxyhaemoglobin

4.2 Transport of Carbon Dioxide

  • Some dissolved in plasma
  • Some transported as bicarbonate ions
  • Some bound to haemoglobin

5. Ventilation (Breathing)

Ventilation is the movement of air into and out of the lungs.

5.1 Inhalation

  1. Diaphragm contracts and flattens
  2. External intercostal muscles contract
  3. Ribs move upwards and outwards
  4. Thoracic cavity volume increases
  5. Pressure decreases
  6. Air moves into the lungs

5.2 Exhalation

  1. Diaphragm relaxes and becomes dome-shaped
  2. External intercostal muscles relax
  3. Ribs move downwards and inwards
  4. Thoracic cavity volume decreases
  5. Pressure increases
  6. Air moves out of the lungs

6. Breathing Mechanism Summary

Inhalation Exhalation
Diaphragm contracts Diaphragm relaxes
Ribs move up and out Ribs move down and in
Volume increases Volume decreases
Pressure decreases Pressure increases

7. Gaseous Exchange in Plants

  • Occurs through stomata in leaves
  • Also occurs through lenticels in stems

Day:

  • CO₂ enters
  • O₂ exits

Night:

  • O₂ enters
  • CO₂ exits

8. Comparison: Gaseous Exchange in Plants vs Animals

Plants Animals
No specialised organs Specialised organs (lungs)
Uses stomata Uses alveoli
Slow process Faster process

9. Effects of Smoking on the Respiratory System

  • Damages cilia
  • Causes mucus build-up
  • Reduces gaseous exchange
  • Leads to diseases such as bronchitis and emphysema

10. Diseases of the Respiratory System

  • Tuberculosis (TB)
  • Asthma
  • Bronchitis
  • Emphysema

11. Importance of Gaseous Exchange

  • Provides oxygen for cellular respiration
  • Removes carbon dioxide
  • Maintains life processes

12. Quick Summary

  • Gas exchange occurs by diffusion
  • Alveoli are the main exchange surface
  • Oxygen is transported by haemoglobin
  • Ventilation controls air movement
